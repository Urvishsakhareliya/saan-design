What is Pashmina, How Pashmina is made? History and People Opinion

  • Blog, Pashmina
  • 4 Comment
Pashmina is derived from the Persian word Pamina which literally means “made of wool”. Pashmina is an Urdu word with a Parsi origin and is the name given to Changthangi goat’s fleece. Pashmina is rare and desired around the 15000 feet above sea level in Ladakh, India. Pashmina has fascinated kings, royals, and people all over the world with its magical allure and traditional elegance. Perhaps, it was for this reason that we chose to demonstrate the exquisiteness and regal demeanour of this centuries-old art to the rest of the world.
